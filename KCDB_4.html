Data Protection Framework
Compliance with Data Protection Act 2019 (Cap. 411C) & Art. 31, Constitution of Kenya
🔐
Right to Privacy (Art. 31)
All personal data processed within the constitutional right to privacy. No private family affairs data collected beyond strict necessity.
⚖️
Lawful & Transparent Processing
Processing only under explicit statutory authority (proposed Part XVII, Children Act 2022). Every access event is logged and auditable.
🎯
Purpose Limitation
Data collected solely for child protection and justice delivery. Cannot be used for immigration enforcement or criminal profiling.
📉
Data Minimisation
UCID contains zero personally identifiable data. Automated minimisation strips identifying data upon discharge.
✅
Accuracy & Right to Correct
Parents/guardians may request correction under S.26(d) DPA 2019. All correction requests are logged and tracked.
🗑️
Storage Limitation
Records retained only while the case is active. On discharge, identifying data is permanently anonymised per S.25 DPA 2019.
🛡️
Integrity & Security
End-to-end encryption, role-based access, immutable audit logs, cryptographic UCID generation. 72-hour breach notification to ODPC.
🌍
No Cross-Border Transfer
No KCDB data transferred outside Kenya without approval from the Data Protection Commissioner and NCCS (S.25(h) DPA 2019).
