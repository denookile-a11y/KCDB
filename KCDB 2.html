Data Protection Notice

UCIDs are cryptographically generated random identifiers. No name, tribal identifier or personally traceable data is embedded. Processing complies with the Data Protection Act 2019 (Cap. 411C) and Article 31, Constitution of Kenya.

Mandatory Registration

Failure to register a qualifying child within 24 hours of a triggering event constitutes a criminal offence under the proposed Part XVII, Children Act 2022.

Data Protection Framework
KCDB compliance with Data Protection Act 2019 (Cap. 411C) & Article 31, Constitution of Kenya
🔐
Right to Privacy (Art. 31)
All personal data processed in accordance with the constitutional right to privacy. No data requiring disclosure of private family affairs is collected beyond what is strictly necessary.
⚖️
Lawful & Transparent Processing
Processing occurs only under explicit statutory authority (proposed Part XVII, Children Act 2022). Every access event is logged and auditable by the NCCS and Data Protection Commissioner.
🎯
Purpose Limitation
Data collected solely for child protection, case management, and justice delivery. It cannot be processed for immigration enforcement, criminal profiling, or any incompatible purpose.
📉
Data Minimisation
UCID contains zero personally identifiable data. Only the minimum necessary case information is stored. Automated minimisation protocols strip identifying data upon discharge.
✅
Accuracy & Right to Correct
Parents, guardians, or authorised representatives may request correction of false or misleading data under S.26(d) DPA 2019. All correction requests are logged and tracked.
🗑️
Storage Limitation & Deletion
Records are retained only for as long as the child's case is active. Upon discharge, identifying data is permanently anonymised. Aggregate statistics only are retained thereafter.
🛡️
Integrity & Security
End-to-end encryption, role-based access control, immutable audit logs, and cryptographic UCID generation. Breach notifications to ODPC within 72 hours as required by law.
🌍
No Cross-Border Transfer
No KCDB data is transferred outside Kenya without approval from the Data Protection Commissioner and NCCS, as required under S.25(h) DPA 2019.
